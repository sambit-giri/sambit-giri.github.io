I am a postdoc working in the field of Cosmic Reionization. My prime interest is to develop tools to study the physics of the universe when the first stars formed and reionized the universe. I also work on Bayesian inference of astronomical data (mostly, spectra of high redshift objects and the 21-cm signal).

Experience

Postdoctoral research

University of Zurich, Zurich
JAN 2020 - present

Currently, we have entered into an age of Big Data in the field astronomy. In order to understand the physics of our universe, we need to match the observations with computer simulations. The simulations that are more accurate are computationally more expensive. Therefore most cosmological inference frameworks have to find a balance between accuracy and speed. I work on methods to build accurate emulators of cosmological simulations, which can be used to interpret observations.

Stockholm University, Stockholm
APR 2019 - DEC 2019

During this period, I studied the topological information contained in the 3D images of 21-cm signal produced during the epoch of reionization. I developed a fast estimator of a topological metric, known as the betti numbers, and used it to understand the topologigy of ionized bubbles.

PhD research

Stockholm University, Stockholm

The present radio telescopes like GMRT, MWA, LOFAR, etc and the future SKA are hopeful of observing the EoR by detecting the 21-cm signal (produced due to spin-flip transition of neutral Hydrogen). SKA will have better sensitivity and will be able to produce images. In preparation for the data produced by SKA, it is prudent to develop tools to analyse and interpret this highly complex signal. I am interested in the non-Gaussian information in this signal and work on using the image processing techniques to study the tomographic images of 21-cm signal.
I am also involved in developing a machine learning package to study the spectra from the galaxies during EoR. This would be very useful in studying the spectra from the future JWST.
